# Safety and correctness fixes

## Summary

Four critical and high-priority fixes identified by the PRD-600 architectural review. These address: (1) a crash-recovery gap in the reconcile function that can strand the repo on a wrong branch, (2) a shell injection surface through PRD frontmatter titles, (3) a type-safety bypass via setattr side channel in the runner, and (4) the absence of linting in CI allowing lint violations to merge undetected.

## Motivation

These are the highest-signal findings from the architectural review's adversarial phase. The reconcile crash recovery and shell injection issues are genuine operational/security risks -- not theoretical. The setattr side channel is a trivial fix that eliminates a known type-safety gap. Ruff in CI is the single highest-impact DevOps improvement.

All four tasks are independent and can be executed in parallel.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] `_create_reconcile_pr` handles partial subprocess failure without stranding the repo
- [ ] User-controlled values in `format_string()` are shell-escaped before `shell=True` execution
- [ ] `_last_agent_result` setattr/getattr pattern replaced with existing typed field
- [ ] CI runs `ruff check` and `ruff format --check` and fails on violations
